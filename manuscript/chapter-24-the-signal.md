# Chapter 24 - The Signal

The mountain trail began with false confidence.

From camp, the route looked reasonable. It rose through trees behind the lake, crossed a band of open rock, then climbed toward the ridge Kevin had pointed out the day before. On the map, it was a clear line. On the ground, it was a negotiation with roots, loose stones, wet salal, and the ongoing fact that Kelsey had used several muscles overnight that hiking now expected to participate without comment.

"How is the pace?" Kevin asked after the first sustained climb.

"Fine."

He glanced back.

"Fine means fine," she said.

"With you, fine sometimes means I am about to be tried in court."

"That was one work email."

"It had attachments."

She laughed, then regretted it because laughing engaged muscles that were already filing reports.

Kevin stopped at a bend where a cedar root made a natural step. "Water."

"You are not subtle."

"Correct."

They drank. The lake appeared through the trees below, smaller now, the tent a barely visible patch of green near shore. Seeing it from above made the night feel both real and improbable. Kelsey touched the strap of her daypack and felt the phone inside, useless without signal. Sam existed somewhere beyond the mountain, beyond the quiet, probably teaching herself not to imagine too much and imagining anyway.

Kelsey wanted to text her.

She also did not.

No signal let her postpone becoming a person who had to translate what happened into words.

They climbed again.

For a while, the trail demanded enough attention to silence everything else. Kevin went first through the steepest sections, offering a hand when the rock was slick, stepping aside when Kelsey wanted to choose her own route. His restraint had changed overnight. It was still there, but it no longer pretended not to know what it was restraining.

At one narrow ledge, his hand settled briefly at her lower back to steady her.

Kelsey looked over her shoulder.

"That is new."

His hand lifted immediately. "Too much?"

"I did not say stop."

He put it back, just for the last step.

The warmth of his palm through her shirt stayed with her for the next hundred metres.

The trees thinned near an open stretch of rock where the trail angled toward a view. Wind hit them harder there, cool and clean with altitude. Kelsey stopped to catch her breath and pulled out her phone to check the time.

Bars appeared.

Then messages.

They came in all at once, stacked bright on the screen.

Sam:

`You alive?`

Kelsey stared.

Another.

Sam:

`Do not make it weird in your head.`

Her throat tightened so suddenly she had to swallow around it.

Kevin noticed from three steps above. "Signal?"

Kelsey nodded but did not look away from the screen.

The next message landed.

Sam:

`How's his cock?`

Kelsey laughed before she could stop herself.

It came out shaky and bright, half relief and half disbelief, and then she was laughing harder, one hand pressed to her mouth while her eyes stung. The laugh had too much in it: fear, love, the ache in her thighs, Sam's unmistakable voice cutting through guilt with a filthy knife.

Kevin came back down two steps. "What happened?"

Kelsey turned the phone enough for him to read it.

His eyes moved over the messages.

He looked away first, smiling into the trees with his whole face transformed by embarrassment and affection.

"That is Sam," he said.

"That is very Sam."

Kelsey sank onto a rock because standing suddenly felt ambitious.

That was the moment she understood Sam was not absent from this. Sam was not fragile glass they were breaking behind her back. Sam was there, blunt and jealous and hilarious and unmistakably herself, giving Kelsey permission in the only way that could have cut through the guilt cleanly.

Not permission to take.

Permission to stop punishing herself for receiving what had been offered with open eyes.

Kelsey wiped under one eye with her thumb.

"I need to answer."

Kevin nodded and stepped away, giving her privacy without going far.

Kelsey typed, deleted, typed again.

`Alive. Not making it weird. Trying.`

She stared at the third message.

Then she added:

`Also, you are a menace.`

Sam's typing dots appeared almost immediately.

`Answer the question, coward.`

Kelsey made a sound that was nearly a sob and nearly another laugh.

She looked at Kevin. He stood near the edge of the rock, facing the view, hands on his hips, pretending with deep commitment not to listen.

Kelsey typed:

`Good. Careful. Big enough that I am walking thoughtfully.`

Her face caught fire the second she sent it.

Sam:

`Proud of you. Hydrate.`

Then:

`Both meanings.`

Kelsey dropped her forehead to her knees.

Kevin looked over. "Should I be afraid?"

"Always."

"Good to know."

She handed him the phone because hiding the conversation would make it heavier than sharing it. He read the new messages, and his ears turned red at the same time his expression softened.

"Walking thoughtfully?" he asked.

"Do not become smug on a mountain."

"I would never."

"You absolutely would."

He gave the phone back. "Are you okay?"

This time the question did not make her feel fragile.

"Better," she said.

"Because of her?"

"Because she is still her."

Kevin nodded, understanding immediately.

Kelsey looked at the message thread again. Sam had sent one more.

`I love you. I am weird and okay. Send mountain proof when possible.`

Kelsey held the phone to her chest.

"She says she is weird and okay."

Kevin exhaled. "That sounds true."

"Do you need to text her?"

"Yes. Later, if that is okay. I don't want to take your signal moment."

"Our signal moment is apparently about your cock."

He closed his eyes. "I deserve none of this."

"You bought the earrings."

"That was before wilderness accountability."

Kelsey laughed again, easier now.

She took a picture of the view: the lake below, the dark line of trees, Kevin's shoulder at the edge of the frame because she did not crop him out in time. She sent it to Sam.

`Mountain proof.`

Sam:

`Good view. Both kinds?`

Kelsey did not answer that one.

Not with text.

She stood, tucked the phone away, and walked to Kevin. He looked at her, still careful, still waiting.

Kelsey put a hand on his chest, rose on her toes, and kissed him in the open air with the lake below them and Sam's messages warm in her pocket.

It was not a hidden kiss. Not from Sam, not from herself.

Kevin's hands came to her waist. He kept the kiss brief because they were on uneven rock and because he was Kevin.

When they separated, Kelsey rested her forehead against his chin.

"Friends with benefits," she said softly.

He went still.

"Not a decision yet," she added. "Just... I think that is the shape. Maybe."

His hands stayed steady at her waist. "Maybe."

"Not romance."

"Okay."

"But not nothing."

"Definitely not nothing."

The words settled between them like a cairn on the trail. Not the whole route. Enough to mark where they stood.

The phone lost signal ten minutes later.

Kelsey did not mind.
