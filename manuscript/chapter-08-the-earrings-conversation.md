# Chapter 8 - The Earrings Conversation

Kelsey noticed the earrings because Sam wanted her to.

Not obviously. Sam did not shake her head around like a woman trying to advertise fruit. She simply wore her hair tucked behind one ear when Kelsey came over for coffee, and she chose the chair where the kitchen window put a clean morning light along her cheek.

Subtle, for Sam.

The little gold pineapple hung upside down.

Kelsey saw it while stirring cream into her coffee. Her spoon touched the side of the mug once, too loud.

Sam's eyes lifted.

"What?" she asked, already smiling.

"Nothing."

"That was not a nothing spoon noise."

Kelsey looked at the earring again. She knew enough internet shorthand to recognize the shape of the question and not enough to ask it gracefully. There were things a person could know casually that became ridiculous when spoken across a best friend's kitchen table.

"Are those..." She stopped.

Sam rested her chin in her hand. "Are they tiny pineapples? Yes."

"Upside down tiny pineapples."

"Very observant."

"Sam."

"Kelsey."

The silence stretched just long enough for Kelsey to feel heat rise under her collar.

Sam's smile gentled. "Ask."

"Is it a joke?"

"Yes."

Kelsey exhaled.

Then Sam added, "And not only a joke."

The room shifted. Not dramatically. The fridge hummed. A gull made an ugly noise somewhere beyond the deck. Kevin was at work, and the house still held him in ordinary traces: boots by the mat, camera battery charging near the outlet, a note on the counter reminding Sam to take the leftovers.

Kelsey wrapped both hands around her mug. "I do not know how to ask without sounding nosy."

"You are my best friend. You are allowed to be nosy if you do not pretend you are above it."

"That is an aggressive standard."

"I learned from you."

Kelsey looked at the earrings again, then at Sam. "Are you and Kevin open?"

Sam did not look away.

"Yes."

The answer was simple enough that Kelsey had nowhere to put all the noise it made in her head.

"Like... actually?"

"Actually."

"Not just joking online-open."

"Not just joking online-open."

Kelsey took a sip of coffee and burned her tongue. "Ow. Good. Great timing."

Sam got up for a glass of water without making the kindness into a production. She set it beside Kelsey's mug and sat again.

"How long?" Kelsey asked.

"A few years in a real way. Longer in conversation. It did not happen because something was missing, if that is the next question."

"It wasn't."

Sam raised one eyebrow.

"It was not the next question I was going to say out loud."

"Better."

Kelsey rubbed her thumb over the mug handle. "So you date people?"

"Sometimes."

"Men?"

"Sometimes."

"Women?"

Sam's smile changed. "Sometimes."

Kelsey looked down too quickly.

That was irritating. Sam was beautiful; Kelsey had eyes and a working nervous system. Noticing her best friend's mouth around a teasing answer did not need to become a personal crisis. It had happened before in harmless flashes: Sam in a low-cut dress, Sam stretching after a run, Sam laughing too close while fixing Kelsey's hair before a wedding. Kelsey had always let those moments pass through her unnamed.

This one stayed because the conversation had given it somewhere to land.

"Kevin knows?" Kelsey asked.

"Kevin knows."

"Before?"

"Before enough to consent. After enough to stay connected. During sometimes, depending on the person and the plan."

Kelsey let that arrange itself. "Does he get jealous?"

"Yes."

The answer surprised her more than the first one.

Sam leaned back in her chair. "I do too. We are not enlightened forest spirits. We get weird, we talk, we make rules, we change rules when they stop working. Sometimes it is hot. Sometimes it hurts. Often it is both."

Kelsey stared into her coffee. "That sounds exhausting."

"Sometimes."

"Why do it?"

Sam thought about the question instead of performing certainty. "Because freedom matters to me. Because honesty matters to both of us. Because wanting someone else does not automatically mean I love Kevin less. Because sometimes seeing him want me after I have been with someone else tells me something about us I do not get any other way."

Kelsey swallowed.

"And Kevin?" she asked.

"Kevin is more selective."

"Meaning?"

"Meaning he does not chase novelty for its own sake. He needs trust. Context. A reason that feels like a person, not just an opportunity."

That was too much information and not enough. Kelsey looked toward the hallway because looking at Sam felt risky.

"Has he..." She stopped.

Sam waited.

"Has he dated anyone I know?"

"No."

Kelsey nodded too fast.

Sam watched her over the rim of her mug. "That was not the question you wanted."

"It was adjacent."

"Kels."

The nickname softened the room and made escape harder.

Kelsey pushed a hand through her hair. "I don't know what I am asking."

"Okay."

"It just changes how I understand things."

"Which things?"

"You. Him. The earrings. Ryan. The way you joke." She looked at Sam finally. "The way you are not jealous in the ways I expected."

Sam's face warmed with something like affection and caution together. "I am jealous in plenty of ways. I am just not jealous by default as a substitute for thinking."

"That sounds like a motivational poster for complicated people."

"I would sell three."

Kelsey laughed, grateful for the release.

They talked for another hour. Sam explained safer-sex rules without making them sound like a policy manual. Short-term partners had stricter boundaries. Testing mattered. Condoms mattered depending on the person, the relationship, and the level of trust. Kevin was not a loophole Sam exploited or a gatekeeper she tricked. They told each other the truth because the arrangement only worked if honesty stayed faster than shame.

Kelsey asked questions she pretended were theoretical.

What if someone catches feelings?

What if one of you likes someone more than expected?

What if the other person is a friend?

Sam answered the last one slowly.

"Then everyone has to be more careful, not less honest."

Kelsey nodded as if she had asked from a distance.

Sam let her have that lie for the length of one breath.

"If you are worried this changes whether you can be around Kevin, it doesn't have to."

Kelsey's face went hot. "I didn't say that."

"No."

"Sam."

"I am not accusing you of anything."

"It feels like you are seeing through my skull."

"That is because you make the same face before lying and before overthinking."

Kelsey put both hands over her face. "I hate this conversation."

Sam laughed softly. "No, you don't."

Kelsey lowered her hands enough to glare.

Sam reached across the table and touched her wrist, brief and steady. "Nothing has to happen because you know more."

That was the first thing all morning that made Kelsey's throat tighten.

"But if knowing more makes you feel something, you can tell me," Sam said. "Even if it is messy. Especially if it is messy."

Kelsey looked at Sam's hand on her wrist, then at the gold pineapple near her jaw, then at Sam's mouth because apparently her self-control had filed a complaint and left the building.

Sam noticed.

Of course she noticed.

She did not move her hand away.

"Kels," she said, quieter.

Kelsey pulled her wrist back gently and reached for her coffee with both hands. "I need to go to work."

"It is Saturday."

"Then I need to go to fake work."

Sam's smile returned, but softer than before. "Text me when your fake shift ends."

Kelsey stood, collected her bag, and made it all the way to the door before turning back.

"Did Kevin buy them?"

Sam touched one earring. "Yes."

"Of course he did."

"He thought I would laugh."

"Did you?"

"Eventually."

Kelsey nodded and stepped outside into air that felt too bright.

She sat in her car for a full minute before starting it.

Knowing more did not mean anything had to happen.

That was true.

It also meant the locked door she had never been willing to touch had not been locked at all.
