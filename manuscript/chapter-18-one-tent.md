# Chapter 18 - One Tent

The tent problem announced itself by smell.

Kelsey noticed first because she was closest when Kevin unrolled the bundle on a flat patch between two cedars. At first she thought it was old lake mud or the sour damp of gear stored too long after rain. Then the fabric opened another fold and the smell came up green, musty, unmistakable.

"Oh no," she said.

Kevin froze.

"What kind of oh no?"

"The biological kind."

He crouched beside the tent and lifted the edge.

Mold marked the inner wall in pale blooms, worse near the corner where the fabric had been rolled tight around a crease. Not a little. Not the kind a person could wipe off and pretend not to think about while breathing inside it all night. The fly had spots too. One seam looked compromised where mildew had eaten at the coating.

Kevin stared at it with the face of a man reviewing every decision that had brought him here.

Kelsey pressed her lips together.

"Do not laugh," he said.

"I am not."

"You are vibrating."

"I am respecting your grief."

"This is my good tent."

"Was."

He looked up.

She lost the fight and laughed.

To his credit, he lasted three seconds before laughing too, though his laugh had more pain in it. He sat back on his heels and dragged a hand over his face.

"I checked the poles."

"Did the poles have mold?"

"No."

"Then your process was flawless except for the entire tent."

"Thank you."

"I support you."

"You are enjoying this."

"A little."

The practical problem took over before the implication could. Kevin examined the fabric, confirmed the damage was not campable, and swore with quiet specificity. Kelsey helped him shake it out away from the sleeping area so spores and dirt did not settle on their other gear. They folded it loosely into a contractor bag he had packed for trash, then lashed it near the edge of camp like evidence.

"I am never telling Sam," Kevin said.

Kelsey looked at him.

He sighed. "I am telling Sam immediately when we have signal."

"She will be compassionate."

"No, she will be funny."

"Same thing, sometimes."

They stood over the ruined tent with the lake behind them and Kelsey's tent still packed.

Only then did the next fact arrive.

One tent.

Not one tent because of desire, planning, fantasy, or anyone's bold decision. One tent because gear stored damp had consequences.

Kevin looked at Kelsey's pack, then at her.

"We can hike out."

The offer came fast. Too fast to be anything but genuine.

Kelsey felt immediate relief and immediate disappointment, which told her more than she wanted to know.

"No," she said.

"We can. We have enough daylight if we leave soon."

"After hiking in, swimming in underwear, and murdering your tent? That sounds miserable."

"Miserable is an option."

"It is not my preferred option."

"Kels."

She looked at him fully. "Do you want to hike out?"

He did not answer with the easy protective lie. His jaw shifted once.

"No."

"Okay."

"But I need you to know it is available."

"I know."

"If the tent situation makes this feel pressured-"

"It doesn't."

He held her gaze.

She corrected herself because honesty deserved precision. "It makes it feel more real. That is not the same thing as pressured."

The wind moved through the trees, bringing the lake smell up the slope. Somewhere across the water, a raven called. Kelsey's damp hair chilled at the nape of her neck despite the dry shirt she had changed into.

Kevin nodded once.

"Then we set up yours," he said.

The practical work saved them again.

Kelsey's tent was smaller than Kevin's had promised to be, a two-person backpacking tent that was technically large enough for two adults if those adults were honest about elbows. They chose the flattest pad, cleared sticks and small stones, laid out the footprint, and worked from opposite sides while the fabric took shape between them.

The tent did not care about sexual tension. It cared about corners, stakes, pole clips, and whether the fly was oriented correctly. Kelsey appreciated that. She could pull fabric tight, hammer a stake with a rock, crawl half-inside to smooth the floor, and let each task hold her attention in its hands.

Until she backed out of the tent and found Kevin watching.

Not crudely. Not even openly, exactly. But his eyes moved from the place where her shirt had ridden up at her waist to her face with the guilty discipline of a good man losing a private battle.

Kelsey sat back on her heels.

"Still okay?" he asked.

She smiled slowly. "You ask that a lot."

"I mean it a lot."

The smile softened.

"Still okay," she said.

They inflated sleeping pads side by side. The pads filled the tent nearly wall to wall. Kelsey's quilt went on one side, Kevin's sleeping bag on the other, their pillows touching because there was nowhere else for them to go. Kevin placed a small lantern near the door, a packet of wipes beside it, and his folded sleep clothes at the foot.

The sight was more intimate than the lake.

Two sleeping places. One thin wall of nylon. No Sam between them, no group chat, no truck cab, no trail bend offering an excuse to look away.

Kevin unzipped the mesh door and backed out.

"I can sleep outside if-"

"Stop."

He did.

Kelsey stood beside him and looked at the tent they had built.

"If I need space, I will tell you," she said. "If you need space, you tell me. If either of us changes our mind about anything, we say it. But do not preemptively punish yourself for a moldy tent."

His mouth curved despite himself. "That is oddly specific."

"It is an oddly specific day."

He looked at the contractor bag containing his ruined tent. "I hate that thing."

"The tent or the metaphor?"

"Both."

She laughed, and the laughter finally let them breathe.

They finished organizing camp with the one-tent fact sitting behind every movement. Food hung from a branch away from camp. The stove area was set near a flat rock. Wet clothes from the lake were draped over low branches where the breeze could reach them. Kevin opened Sam's note at last while Kelsey filtered water.

He read it silently, then handed it to her.

Sam had written:

`If the tent collapses, blame Kevin. If feelings collapse, drink water before discussing. If clothes come off, remember I love you both and I am absolutely asking questions later.`

Kelsey laughed until her eyes stung.

Kevin folded the note carefully and tucked it into his pack.

The tent stood behind them, small and green and unavoidable.

They did not need to say what it meant.

Not yet.
