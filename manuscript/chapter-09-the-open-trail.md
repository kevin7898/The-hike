# Chapter 9 - The Open Trail

The next solo hike with Kevin was arranged in the group chat, which made Kelsey feel both safer and more ridiculous.

Sam began it.

`I am declaring myself unavailable for sunrise crimes this weekend.`

Kevin answered, `Neck Point is not a crime.`

`It is if the alarm begins with a 4.`

Kelsey typed nothing.

Then Sam added, `Kels, go with him so he stops trying to recruit me like a cult.`

Kevin:

`This cult has coffee.`

Kelsey stared at the screen long enough for it to dim. The old version of the invitation would have been simple. Sam did not want to get up; Kevin wanted sunrise photos; Kelsey liked trails. Friendship, logistics, weather.

Now she knew about the earrings.

Now she knew Kevin and Sam were open, which did not mean Kevin wanted her, did not mean Sam wanted that, did not mean Kelsey was allowed to turn every ordinary kindness into a possibility.

It simply meant the impossibility had been removed.

That was worse.

She wrote, `Fine, but I require coffee and no cult robes.`

Sam replied with a string of celebratory emojis.

Kevin replied, `Deal.`

Saturday came clear and cold, the sky dark enough when Kevin arrived that the porch light made his truck look suspended in black glass. Kelsey stepped outside with her pack and immediately regretted not adding another layer.

Kevin leaned across to open the passenger door from inside.

"Morning."

"That is unconfirmed."

He handed her a travel mug. "Coffee."

"Evidence in your favour."

The drive was quiet at first. Kelsey held the warm mug with both hands and watched familiar streets roll by under streetlights. Kevin had the heat turned up. His camera bag sat on the back seat beside a folded jacket, and the cab smelled faintly of coffee, clean fabric, and whatever pine-scented thing Sam had clipped to the vent despite Kevin's protests.

"Sam told you about the earrings," he said.

Kelsey almost spilled coffee on herself.

"Wow. Direct."

"Sorry."

"No, it is fine." She looked out the windshield. "Yes. She told me."

"Okay."

One word. No pressure behind it.

Kelsey waited for him to explain, apologize, joke, ask what she thought, turn the knowledge into a conversation she had not prepared for.

He did none of that.

The restraint made her more aware of him than questions would have.

At the trailhead, they moved into the practical work of hiking: zippers, straps, headlamps, the first cold breath as they stepped out from the truck. The path was darker under trees than Kelsey expected, the gravel patched with puddles from rain two nights before. Kevin walked beside her where the trail allowed and ahead where it narrowed.

She noticed everything.

The flex of his hand around the camera strap. The way he slowed without announcing it when roots crossed the path. The brief glance back to make sure she saw a low branch. His refusal to make any of those gestures meaningful by naming them.

Kelsey had thought flirting would be the dangerous thing.

This was worse: patience with no claim attached.

They reached the first overlook as the eastern sky began to pale. The water was flat and dark below them, the rock slick enough that Kevin pointed without touching.

"Careful there."

"I have feet."

"I have seen evidence."

"Strong observational work."

He smiled and let her choose her own path down.

That mattered too.

When they stopped, Kelsey stood a little closer than she needed to. Not shoulder to shoulder. Nothing that would require either of them to respond. Just close enough that she could feel the heat of him on one side when the wind came off the water.

Kevin lifted his camera.

"You can tell me if this is weird," he said.

Her pulse tripped. "The hike?"

"The openness thing."

Kelsey kept her eyes on the horizon. "Is it weird for you?"

"Less than pretending you don't know."

That was honest enough to steady her.

"I am still arranging it in my head," she said.

"That makes sense."

"Sam is very Sam about it."

"She usually is."

"She said jealousy is not a substitute for thinking."

Kevin lowered the camera and laughed under his breath. "She has been workshopping that one."

"It sounded rehearsed."

"It came out of an argument about a bartender in Victoria."

Kelsey turned her head. "Do I want to know?"

"Probably not before sunrise."

The conversation could have opened there. Kelsey could have asked whether he dated, whether he wanted to, whether friends were impossible or simply complicated. Instead, the light changed, and Kevin's attention moved with it.

He took three pictures in quick succession.

Kelsey watched his face while he looked through the viewfinder. Focus had a physical effect on him. It stilled the casual humour, sharpened the line of his mouth, made every movement deliberate. She remembered Sam saying Kevin needed trust, context, a reason that felt like a person.

Kelsey looked away.

On the return, the trail felt different because daylight had made it ordinary and Kelsey's thoughts had not followed. Kevin walked ahead on a downhill section where the ground was slick with needles. His shoulders moved under his jacket. His boots found stable places before hers did.

"Root," he said.

"I see it."

"Second one hides."

She did not see it until he pointed, and by then she was close enough that he offered a hand.

Again, she could have managed alone.

Again, she took it.

This time, when she reached the level ground, he did not release immediately. Not long. Half a beat too much, or exactly enough for both of them to know the difference.

His thumb rested against the side of her hand.

Then he let go.

Kelsey kept walking because stopping would have made the moment stand trial.

"Thanks," she said.

"Yeah."

They said little for the next five minutes.

At the truck, Kevin poured the last of his coffee into the lid of the thermos and offered it to her.

"Communal germs?" she asked.

"Trail cult standard."

She took it.

The coffee was lukewarm and too strong. She drank anyway, aware of his eyes on the side of her face and aware that he looked away when she turned.

On the drive home, Sam texted.

`Did the sun perform?`

Kelsey answered in the group chat with a picture Kevin had sent her from the overlook.

`Annoyingly, yes.`

Sam:

`Good. I contributed by remaining warm.`

Kevin:

`Your sacrifice is noted.`

Kelsey looked at the exchange and felt something loosen. Sam was still there. Not in the truck, not on the trail, but present in the shape of the morning. Teasing. Knowing. Unafraid enough to joke, or choosing to joke because she was not entirely unafraid.

Kelsey did not know which.

That mattered too.

When Kevin pulled up in front of her house, he left the truck running.

"Kels."

She turned with her hand on the door.

He seemed to choose each word before letting it out. "I am not going to push anything because you know more now."

Her throat tightened.

"Okay."

"But I also don't want to pretend nothing changed if it did."

The sentence hung between them, careful and enormous.

Kelsey nodded once. "Okay."

He accepted that as enough.

She stepped out into the cold morning, closed the door, and watched his truck pull away.

Only when she was inside with her back against the front door did she let herself say the truth silently.

Something had changed.
