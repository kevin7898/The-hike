# Chapter 31 - The Drive Home

The truck smelled like damp gear, sunscreen, smoke, trail food, and tired bodies trying to become acceptable to society.

Kelsey sat in the passenger seat with one foot tucked under her, hair pulled into a messy knot, hands wrapped around a gas-station coffee Kevin had bought even though they both knew it would be bad. The first sip confirmed everything. She drank it anyway because warmth mattered more than quality.

For the first twenty minutes, they said almost nothing.

The road unwound through trees and cutblocks, then widened toward town. Kelsey watched the forest thin and felt the trip turning into memory before she was ready. The lake, the tent, the summit, the fire, all of it receding behind the truck with the same physical inevitability as the kilometres.

Kevin kept one hand on the wheel and the other resting near the gearshift.

Not reaching.

Available.

After a long stretch of silence, Kelsey set her coffee in the cup holder and placed her hand over his.

His fingers turned immediately under hers.

They drove like that until the road required more attention and he needed both hands for a curve. He squeezed once before letting go.

"We should not pretend it was casual," Kelsey said.

The sentence had been building since the trailhead and still sounded too abrupt.

Kevin glanced at her, then back to the road. "No."

"I don't mean dramatic. I just mean... if either of us starts saying it was just camping and sex happened, I am going to become a problem."

"Noted."

"A spreadsheet problem."

"Serious."

She smiled, then looked out the window. "It mattered."

"Yes."

"It was intense."

"Yes."

"And I still do not want to make it a relationship just because intensity feels like it demands a bigger box."

Kevin did not answer immediately. The pause was long enough for fear to lift its head, but not long enough to become panic.

"I don't want to put you in the wrong box," he said.

Kelsey closed her eyes.

"Thank you."

"I also don't want to pretend I will not have feelings."

She opened them.

He kept his gaze on the road. "Not girlfriend feelings, necessarily. Not replace-Sam feelings. But feelings. Care. Wanting. Missing you. Worrying if I hurt you. Looking forward to seeing you. Those are real."

Kelsey's throat tightened.

"I have those too."

"Okay."

"Okay?"

"That seems better than pretending friends with benefits means friends with no consequences."

She laughed softly. "That is not a catchy acronym."

"FWNC."

"Terrible."

"Needs work."

The conversation settled, and for a few minutes the only sounds were tires on road and the faint rattle of something in the truck bed.

"Sam said the friends part has to be real," Kelsey said.

"She is right."

"She also said she might need space for fifteen minutes and then text something terrible."

"Also likely."

Kelsey smiled, then felt it fade. "Do you think she is okay?"

Kevin's hands tightened once on the wheel. "I think she is okay and not okay. Both."

"That sounds like her."

"It sounds like all of us."

They stopped at a viewpoint on impulse, not because the view was better than the summit but because neither of them seemed ready to arrive at Kelsey's house with the conversation still folded too tightly. The pullout overlooked a stretch of forest and distant water under afternoon haze. Cars passed behind them. The air smelled like hot dust and salal.

Kelsey leaned against the truck beside Kevin.

"What do you need from me?" she asked.

He looked surprised.

"Eventually," she clarified. "Not this second. But if we try this."

Kevin considered it with the seriousness she had come to trust.

"Honesty faster than avoidance."

"Hard."

"Yes."

"What else?"

"Do not use Sam as a shield if you are actually scared of me."

That one landed.

She nodded slowly. "Okay."

"And tell me if the sex starts feeling like it costs too much afterward."

Kelsey looked at him.

"I can do that," she said.

"You?"

She turned toward the view. "Do not make me manage whether you are secretly hoping it becomes more."

"I can do that."

"Do not get so careful that I have to drag desire out of you with evidence."

His mouth curved. "Working on it."

"And Sam stays in the loop. Not with every detail unless that is what everyone wants, but no secret version."

"Agreed."

She took a breath. "Also I need to go home first. Shower. Panic privately for a little while. Then I want to see her."

"Not me?"

The question was gentle, but not neutral.

Kelsey stepped closer and took his hand. "You later. Her first."

Kevin nodded. No flinch. Maybe a little ache, but no flinch.

"Good," he said.

"Good?"

"Yes. Good."

The relief of being understood made her angry for half a second, because she had braced for the wrong thing and had nowhere to put the unused defense.

So she kissed him.

It was a brief kiss beside the truck in daylight with traffic passing behind them and no lake, no tent, no summit to make it mythic. That was why it mattered. His hand came to her waist, familiar now, then released when she stepped back.

"Still friends?" he asked.

She smiled. "Very."

"Benefits?"

"Likely."

"Likely?"

"Do not get greedy in a viewpoint parking lot."

"Noted."

They got back in the truck.

As they approached Nanaimo, Kelsey's phone woke fully into signal. Messages arrived. Sam, mostly. Some practical. Some ridiculous. One that just said:

`Home-shaped soon?`

Kelsey answered:

`Soon. I want to shower and then come over. Just us first?`

The typing dots appeared, vanished, appeared again.

`Yes. Just us. Love you.`

Kelsey held the phone against her chest and let herself breathe.

When Kevin pulled up outside her house, he did not get out immediately.

"Text me after?" he asked.

"Yes."

"No rush."

"I know."

She gathered her small bag from the cab. The rest of the gear could wait five minutes because every task now felt symbolic and ridiculous.

Before she closed the door, Kevin said, "Kels."

She looked back.

"Thank you for telling me the true shape, even before it is finished."

The words nearly undid her.

"Do not be poetic when I smell this bad."

He smiled. "Driveway rule."

"Good."

She closed the door, watched him pull away, and stood for a moment in front of her own house with the trip on her skin and Sam waiting on the other side of a shower.
