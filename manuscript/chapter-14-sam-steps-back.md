# Chapter 14 - Sam Steps Back

Sam told Kevin first.

She waited until after dinner, after the dishes were done and Aspen had been walked around the block in a drizzle that offended everyone involved. Kevin was wiping rain from the dog's paws in the entryway when Sam leaned against the wall and said, "I think I should back out of the trip."

Kevin looked up.

Aspen took advantage of the pause to place one damp foot directly on his knee.

"Out," he said, though not to the dog.

Sam nodded.

He finished with Aspen because practical care gave his hands somewhere to go. Then he stood, tossed the towel toward the laundry basket, missed, and did not pick it up.

"Because of Kelsey?"

"Because of all three of us."

The house was quiet except for the rain ticking against the front window. Sam had changed into leggings and one of Kevin's hoodies. The pineapple earrings were not in her ears tonight. That made her look younger somehow, or maybe only less armoured.

"I am not giving you away," she said.

Kevin flinched. "I know."

"Do you?"

He leaned back against the wall opposite her. "No. Not completely."

Sam's expression softened. "Good. Then we can have the real conversation."

They moved to the kitchen table because serious talks in the bedroom became other things too easily and serious talks standing in hallways made both of them defensive. Sam made tea neither of them really wanted. Kevin sat with his hands around the mug and listened while she found the right shape of the truth.

"Something is already happening," she said. "Even if nobody touches anybody. Even if we all behave so well we deserve certificates. You and Kelsey have a line between you now."

Kevin did not deny it.

"If I go," Sam continued, "I become the person watching you both pretend the line isn't there. Or managing it. Or making jokes every time it gets too visible so no one has to deal with it. That sounds exhausting and also a little mean."

"Mean to who?"

"All of us."

He looked at the steam rising from his mug. "You want me to go with her alone."

"I want to make room for an honest choice. That is not the same as wanting every possible outcome."

The distinction was important. It was also where the jealousy lived.

Kevin reached across the table.

Sam gave him her hand.

"Tell me the hard part," he said.

She laughed once without humour. "The hard part is that I can want it and hate it in the same minute."

"Yeah."

"I am turned on by the idea. I am proud of her for wanting something after everything with Mark made her shrink. I trust you more than I trust anyone. And also, when I picture you looking at her the way you look when you stop trying not to want something, I want to throw a plate."

Kevin's thumb moved over her knuckles.

"Please don't."

"I like our plates."

"Me too."

The joke helped because it did not erase anything.

They talked through the parts that needed talking, not as fantasy but as logistics with consequences. Testing first. Recent results visible, not vaguely promised. Kelsey's long dry spell after a relationship that had left her wary of being touched by anyone who wanted too much and listened too little. Condoms available even if the eventual baseline became no-condom with explicit agreement. No surprises. No assuming that Sam's support meant Kelsey had agreed to anything. No treating the woods as a loophole where normal care could be left in the truck.

"If she wants no condoms," Sam said, "it has to be because she wants that. Not because you and I have that, not because she thinks saying yes proves trust."

"I know."

"Say it anyway."

Kevin met her eyes. "If she wants no condoms, it has to be her choice, after testing, with the option to change her mind at any point."

Sam nodded.

"And if she wants nothing," Kevin said, "then nothing happens."

"And you do not come home noble and secretly resentful."

"I will come home tired and probably smelling bad."

"That is already your brand."

He smiled, then sobered. "Are you sure?"

Sam looked toward the dark window. Her reflection looked back, pale and uncertain over the black glass.

"No," she said. "But I am choosing it."

The next evening, Sam went to Kelsey's house with a bottle of wine she had no intention of opening unless the conversation needed a prop.

Kelsey answered the door in joggers and a tank top, hair twisted into a messy knot, face bare. She looked at the bottle, then at Sam.

"This feels ominous."

"It is emotionally flexible."

"Come in."

Sam followed her to the kitchen. Kelsey's camping gear was spread across the dining table in neat, nervous rows. The black quick-dry underwear sat folded near the socks. Sam noticed and did not mention it immediately because she was a merciful woman when properly motivated.

"I am not coming on the trip," Sam said.

Kelsey went still.

"What?"

"I am stepping back."

"Sam."

"Let me finish before your face does that thing."

"My face is doing nothing."

"Your face is filing legal objections."

Kelsey crossed her arms. "Why?"

Sam set the wine on the counter. "Because if I come, all three of us will spend the whole trip negotiating around what you and Kevin are not saying. I love you too much to make you perform innocence for my comfort."

Kelsey's mouth parted, then closed.

"And I love Kevin too much to ask him to pretend restraint is the same thing as honesty," Sam added.

Kelsey looked away. Her throat moved.

"I don't want to hurt you."

"I know."

"No, Sam. I really don't."

Sam stepped closer but did not touch her yet. "I know."

"This is insane."

"It is not insane. It is complicated."

"That is what people say when something is insane but has paperwork."

Sam smiled despite herself. "There is some paperwork."

Kelsey laughed, then covered her face with both hands. The laugh broke wrong at the end.

Sam pulled her into a hug.

For a few seconds, Kelsey stood stiff. Then she folded into Sam hard enough that Sam felt the fear in it.

"You are not losing me," Sam said against her hair.

Kelsey shook her head.

"You are not taking him from me."

Kelsey's breath hitched.

"And if you go and nothing happens, I will still love you. If you go and something happens, I will still love you. I may be weird. I may ask blunt questions. I may need space for fifteen minutes and then text you something terrible. But I am not going to vanish because you wanted something."

Kelsey held on tighter.

When they finally separated, Kelsey wiped under one eye with the heel of her hand.

"You are being too good about this."

"I am being medium-good and very brave."

"I am serious."

"So am I." Sam touched Kelsey's wrist. "I am jealous. I am turned on. I am worried. I am curious. I am not a permission machine, but I am giving you my blessing because I trust you both to keep choosing honestly."

Kelsey's gaze dropped to Sam's mouth for one unguarded second.

Sam noticed and let the moment breathe.

Kelsey looked away first.

"There is another conversation in there too," Sam said gently. "Not tonight unless you want it."

Kelsey shook her head, cheeks flushed. "Not tonight."

"Okay."

They opened the wine after all and talked through testing, boundaries, what Kevin knew, what Kelsey could ask for, what she never had to offer just because the space existed. Sam made three jokes filthy enough to make Kelsey threaten to make her leave. Kelsey asked whether Kevin expected anything.

"Kevin expects to carry too much gear and overthink," Sam said. "Everything else is a choice."

After Sam left, Kelsey stood alone in the quiet kitchen.

The camping pile looked different.

She checked her test results in her health portal, then forwarded what needed forwarding after staring at the screen for five full minutes. She added condoms to the side pocket of her pack because having them mattered even if not using them might matter later. She repacked her clothes, removing an old sports bra and replacing it with the black one that made her stand straighter.

The underwear was last.

Practical pairs first. Then the green-label ones Sam had recommended. Then the black pair from the store.

She held another pair in her hand, soft, dark red, less practical than anything a person needed in the woods.

For a moment, she imagined Kevin never seeing them. She imagined him seeing them. She imagined choosing either outcome without apologizing for the fact that she had prepared.

She packed them.

The decision sat at the bottom of the dry bag, folded and quiet, waiting for the trip to begin.
